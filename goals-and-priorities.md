# Goals and Priorities

## Primary Aim

Build a coherent group of businesses and initiatives that compounds value over time through strong operating discipline, reusable data and technology capability, and selective concentration behind the best growth opportunities.

## Current Priorities

### 1. Scale Caterlytix as the Core Growth Engine

Caterlytix is the highest-leverage commercial opportunity in the group. It should be positioned to win larger hospitality and contract catering groups, expand internationally, and turn Trac Core plus Trac ODM into a repeatable platform rather than a collection of bespoke integrations.

### 2. Keep TAC Operations Strong but Focused

TAC Operations should continue to provide finance, operations, HR, back-office capability, and innovation capacity across the group. Its role is to increase specialist quality and decision velocity, not to blur the operating identity or culture of the businesses it supports.

### 3. Build Group-Level Data and AI Advantage

Where systems are fragmented, standardise the integration and data layer first. Use that foundation to support reporting, workflow applications, and LLM-enabled services without hard-coding one-off solutions into each business.

### 4. Strengthen the Commercial Paths of Pebble and ArroCMS

Pebble and ArroCMS should each be managed against clear market realities. Pebble needs to keep serving schools effectively while creating data interoperability with Caterlytix where useful. ArroCMS should move from emerging product to credible scale-up through sharper positioning, repeatable delivery, and disciplined SaaS growth.

### 5. Use Green Box Ventures as Long-Term Capital Infrastructure

Green Box Ventures should remain the family investment vehicle that accumulates value through TAC Operations shareholding exposure, management charges, and dividends, while preserving capacity for future investment activity.

### 6. Use AGENTSdotNE to Extend Reach and Opportunity

AGENTSdotNE should build regional AI capability, surface talent, and create adjacent innovation opportunities. It is strategically useful because it expands network, credibility, and idea flow, even where direct commercial return is secondary.

## Strategic Principles

- Keep the businesses distinct in market positioning and operational accountability.
- Centralise specialist capability where shared strength matters.
- Do not centralise local delivery ownership or culture by default.
- Standardise at the integration and data layer before standardising customer-facing workflows.
- Prefer recurring SaaS revenue, but use services where they accelerate adoption or strategic access.
- Make platform decisions that increase optionality across the group.

## Things To Avoid

- Treating TAC Operations as a command-and-control parent for every delivery decision.
- Allowing bespoke client work to break Caterlytix platform discipline.
- Building AI features without a clear data, workflow, or commercial advantage.
- Letting emerging initiatives consume disproportionate leadership time without a route to strategic value.
