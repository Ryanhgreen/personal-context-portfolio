# Caterlytix

## Role in the Group

Caterlytix is the primary strategic growth engine in the group. It serves contract caterers and hospitality businesses that need fragmented systems connected, operational data structured, and practical tooling built on top of that foundation.

## Core Offer

- Trac Core acts as middleware across fragmented client systems
- Trac ODM acts as the operational data manager with client tenancy
- The platform supports reporting, applications, and LLM-enabled solutions
- Revenue combines SaaS with professional services

## Strategic Importance

Caterlytix has the clearest route to scale because it can turn difficult integration problems into reusable platform capability. That creates room for enterprise reporting, workflow applications, and AI-enabled products without rebuilding the same logic client by client.

## Market Direction

- Focus on contract caterers and hospitality companies
- Target larger groups over time, including organisations such as Compass
- Build for international growth rather than a purely domestic footprint
- Keep the proposition strong enough for complex enterprise environments

## Operating Principles

- Standardise at the integration and data layer
- Avoid unnecessary standardisation of front-end operating patterns where clients differ materially
- Use services work to accelerate adoption, but protect platform discipline
- Treat data quality, tenancy, and reuse as strategic assets rather than technical details

## Relationship to Other Businesses

- Receives shared operating and innovation support from TAC Operations
- Can consume or expose data relevant to Pebble where there is clear commercial value
- Benefits from broader group AI capability and strategic technology leadership

## What An AI Should Keep In Mind

- Caterlytix decisions should usually be judged by repeatability, enterprise fit, and long-term platform leverage.
- Custom work is acceptable when it strengthens reusable capability or strategic access.
- The integration and data layer is the real control point in the model.
