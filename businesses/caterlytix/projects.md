# Caterlytix Projects

## Trac Core Standardisation

### Strategic Aim

Make Trac Core the standard middleware layer for connecting fragmented client systems without turning each implementation into a separate product line.

### Current Focus

- Define repeatable integration patterns
- Reduce one-off connector logic where a shared pattern is possible
- Improve implementation speed for new client environments

### Why It Matters

Trac Core only becomes a true platform if integration capability can be reused across clients and geographies. Standardisation at this layer is what makes scale realistic.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Pressure to deliver bespoke integrations quickly
- Variability in client source systems
- Need for clear product boundaries between reusable middleware and custom services

## Trac ODM Evolution

### Strategic Aim

Evolve Trac ODM into a robust operational data manager with clear client tenancy, clean data models, and reliable support for downstream reporting and applications.

### Current Focus

- Strengthen tenancy design
- Improve data structure and governance
- Ensure the model supports both operational use and analytical outputs

### Why It Matters

If the operational data layer is weak, reporting, applications, and AI outputs will all become brittle. Trac ODM is central to platform credibility.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Inconsistent upstream data quality
- Model complexity driven by client-specific edge cases
- Need for a disciplined schema and change-management approach

## AI and Application Layer

### Strategic Aim

Build reporting tools, workflow applications, and LLM-enabled services on top of the Trac Core and Trac ODM foundation, rather than as disconnected experiments.

### Current Focus

- Identify high-value workflow and insight use cases
- Prioritise applications that depend on the shared data layer
- Keep AI features tied to operational or commercial outcomes

### Why It Matters

The AI and application layer is where Caterlytix can move from integration partner to strategic platform. The value comes from solving live operational problems, not from adding generic AI features.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Weak data foundations undermining output quality
- Building feature demos without customer pull
- Over-investing in novelty rather than operational value

## Blackpool Council

### Strategic Aim

Use the Blackpool Council work as a credible operating example of how Caterlytix can handle complex real-world environments and produce useful outputs from difficult underlying systems.

### Current Focus

- Deliver reliable outcomes
- Capture reusable patterns and lessons
- Use the engagement to strengthen future enterprise positioning

### Why It Matters

Referenceable delivery in a complex environment can sharpen product direction, improve credibility, and inform what should become standard capability.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Delivery complexity overwhelming platform discipline
- Reuse lessons not being captured properly
- Account-specific needs distorting broader roadmap choices

## Enterprise Growth

### Strategic Aim

Win larger hospitality and contract catering groups by proving that Caterlytix can support enterprise-scale integration, data management, reporting, and applications.

### Current Focus

- Sharpen enterprise value proposition
- Build confidence in scale, tenancy, and governance
- Prioritise opportunities with strong expansion potential

### Why It Matters

Enterprise growth is the route to larger recurring revenue, stronger strategic accounts, and better platform economics.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Product readiness not matching enterprise expectations
- Sales narrative being stronger than delivery capability
- Resource stretch between existing delivery and growth work

## International Expansion

### Strategic Aim

Position Caterlytix to operate beyond the UK and support international clients without rebuilding its model from scratch in each market.

### Current Focus

- Identify which parts of the proposition travel well
- Test platform assumptions against international operating contexts
- Ensure architecture and operating model are not UK-specific by default

### Why It Matters

International expansion increases market size and strategic upside, but only if the platform is portable and the proposition remains coherent outside the domestic context.

### Key Risks and Dependencies

- Hidden localisation assumptions in integrations or workflows
- Insufficient delivery capacity for expansion
- Entering markets before the core platform is standardised enough
