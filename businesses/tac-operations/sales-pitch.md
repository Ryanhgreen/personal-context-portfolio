# Sales Pitch - TAC Operations

## Brand Positioning

TAC Operations is the central enablement layer of the group, providing leadership, shared services, specialist capability, and innovation support that helps the portfolio operate more effectively.

## Who We Help

- sub-organisations within the group
- portfolio leaders needing shared support
- future businesses joining or working closely with the group
- strategic partners seeking operational capability

## Ideal Customer Profiles

- group businesses needing finance, HR, operational, or innovation support
- early-stage or scaling ventures that benefit from shared services
- organisations looking for specialist capability without building it all in-house

## Customer Personas

### Managing Director or Business Lead
Needs support without losing autonomy.

### Functional Lead
Needs stronger finance, HR, or operational infrastructure.

### Founder or Venture Lead
Wants access to central expertise to accelerate growth.

## Problems We Solve

- duplicated overhead across multiple businesses
- lack of central operational control and support
- weak back-office systems
- difficulty scaling leadership and specialist capability
- innovation projects lacking a structured home

## Core Value Proposition

TAC Operations provides the shared operating backbone of the group, improving efficiency, strengthening leadership, and enabling specialist capability to be deployed where it adds most value.

## Why We Win

- combines leadership with practical operational capability
- supports businesses without needing to control them
- creates shared standards and support across the group
- helps specialist resource go further

## Key Sales Messages

- We help businesses scale without duplicating everything.
- Shared services should enable progress, not slow it down.
- Specialist capability is more powerful when it can be deployed across multiple entities.

## Supporting Proof Points

- runs finance, operations, back-office operations, HR, and innovation projects
- home for the CEO and CTO
- designed to build a central team of specialists for the group

## Common Objections

- "Will this create central bottlenecks?"
- "Will this reduce autonomy in the sub-organisations?"
- "Is this a cost centre or a real enabler?"

## Questions to Qualify a Prospect

- Where is operational friction currently slowing growth?
- Which services are currently duplicated or underpowered?
- What specialist capability is missing today?
- Would shared support improve efficiency without damaging autonomy?
- Is there leadership appetite for a group operating model?

## Buying Triggers

- rapid growth creating operational strain
- duplicated back-office costs
- weak functional capability in finance, HR, or operations
- need for a central innovation engine

## Cross-Sell or Group Synergies

- supports all sub-organisations
- can accelerate execution in Caterlytix, Pebble, and ArroCMS
- can make future ventures more viable by reducing setup overhead
