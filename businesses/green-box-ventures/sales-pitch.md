# Sales Pitch - Green Box Ventures

## Brand Positioning

Green Box Ventures is a family investment vehicle focused on building long-term value through disciplined ownership, reinvestment, and portfolio growth.

## Who We Help

- portfolio businesses within the group
- future founders or operators aligned with the group
- potential co-investment or strategic partners

## Ideal Customer Profiles

This is not a traditional sales-led business. The most relevant counterparties are:
- founders seeking aligned capital and strategic support
- businesses with strong fit to the group’s capabilities
- partners looking for long-term, values-aligned investment relationships

## Customer Personas

### Founder or Operator
Looking for patient capital, practical support, and strategic alignment.

### Strategic Partner
Interested in collaboration, co-investment, or long-term portfolio growth.

## Problems We Solve

- lack of patient, strategically aligned capital
- fragmented ownership thinking
- short-term investment decision-making
- limited reinvestment discipline within smaller groups

## Core Value Proposition

Green Box Ventures provides a long-term capital vehicle that captures value from the group and reinvests it into future opportunities, portfolio growth, and family wealth creation.

## Why We Win

- long-term outlook rather than short-term extraction
- close alignment with the operating realities of the group
- ability to reinvest into areas with strategic fit
- disciplined ownership logic

## Key Sales Messages

- We are building for long-term value, not short-term churn.
- Capital should strengthen the portfolio, not distract it.
- We back opportunities that align with our strengths and strategic direction.

## Supporting Proof Points

- holds shareholding in TAC Operations
- intended as the capital and reinvestment layer of the group
- designed to capture returns and redeploy them sensibly over time

## Common Objections

- "This sounds more structural than active."
- "Is this an investment business yet, or just a holding company?"
- "What makes this more than an ownership wrapper?"

## Questions to Qualify a Prospect

- Is there strategic fit with the group’s sectors or capabilities?
- Does this opportunity create long-term value?
- Is the capital requirement sensible relative to likely returns?
- Would this strengthen the portfolio or fragment it?
- Is the leadership aligned with a long-term approach?

## Buying Triggers

- opportunity to deploy retained profits into new ventures
- attractive acquisition or investment opportunities
- new portfolio ideas with strong strategic fit
- desire to diversify and build family wealth deliberately

## Cross-Sell or Group Synergies

- TAC Operations can provide operating support
- portfolio businesses may provide market access or capability
- Green Box Ventures can fund growth across the group
