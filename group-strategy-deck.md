# Group Strategy Deck

## Purpose

This file is a markdown version of the core strategy deck narrative for the group. It is designed to help AI tools draft board-style summaries, strategy papers, investor notes, and internal planning material.

## Slide 1: Group Thesis

Build a coherent group of businesses and initiatives that compounds value through strong shared operating capability, reusable data and technology infrastructure, and concentration behind the strongest growth opportunities.

## Slide 2: Group Structure

- Green Box Ventures is the capital and ownership layer
- TAC Operations is the shared operating and leadership layer
- Caterlytix is the primary strategic growth engine
- Pebble is the direct-to-school product business
- ArroCMS is the training and programme management SaaS platform
- AGENTSdotNE is the ecosystem and innovation layer

## Slide 3: Strategic Logic

The group works because ownership, operating capability, and commercial execution are separated but connected. Shared specialist support improves quality and speed. Distinct businesses preserve market fit. Platform and data capability create leverage across the portfolio.

## Slide 4: Primary Growth Bet

Caterlytix is the main growth platform because it can solve fragmented-system problems at scale. Trac Core provides middleware. Trac ODM provides the operational data layer. Reporting, applications, and LLM-enabled solutions sit on top. This creates a route to repeatable enterprise value rather than one-off delivery work.

## Slide 5: Supporting Businesses

- Pebble provides direct product relationships in schools through Tali and Till
- ArroCMS provides a broader SaaS platform for training and programme management
- TAC Operations provides finance, operations, HR, back-office capability, and innovation support

These businesses and capabilities should support the group strategy without losing their own commercial discipline.

## Slide 6: Design Rules

- centralise shared specialist capability where it adds leverage
- do not centralise customer delivery ownership by default
- standardise at the integration and data layer first
- use AI where it improves workflow, insight, or product value
- preserve clear boundaries between ownership, influence, and direct control

## Slide 7: Near-Term Priorities

- scale Caterlytix with stronger platform discipline
- mature Trac Core and Trac ODM as reusable infrastructure
- strengthen the commercial path of Pebble and ArroCMS
- keep TAC Operations effective without turning it into a bottleneck
- use AGENTSdotNE to expand network, visibility, and innovation opportunity

## Slide 8: What Good Looks Like

The group should become more valuable because:

- growth is concentrated behind the best opportunities
- shared capability improves execution quality across the portfolio
- the data and platform layer becomes more reusable
- businesses remain distinct enough to stay commercially credible
- AI is used as an operational advantage rather than a superficial feature
