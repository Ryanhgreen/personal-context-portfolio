# Group Sales Narrative

## Purpose

This file explains how the group should be described in commercial and relationship-led contexts. It is not a script to be repeated word for word. It is a practical narrative for positioning the group clearly with prospects, partners, and stakeholders.

## Core Narrative

The group combines long-term ownership, shared operating capability, and product-led commercial businesses. That matters because it allows the businesses to operate with sharper specialist support, stronger technical leverage, and a clearer route from operational problem to scalable solution.

TAC Operations provides the shared operating and leadership capability across the group. Caterlytix is the primary growth engine for hospitality and contract catering, using Trac Core and Trac ODM to turn fragmented systems into a usable operational and data foundation. Pebble serves schools directly with Tali and Till. ArroCMS is the group’s training and programme management platform, built around communications and AI. AGENTSdotNE strengthens regional visibility, talent flow, and innovation reach.

## How To Position The Group

When presenting the group, emphasise:

- there is shared capability behind the businesses, not isolated point solutions
- each business still has its own market logic and commercial focus
- technical and data capability is treated as strategic infrastructure, not just delivery support
- the group is designed to create leverage across operations, products, data, and AI

## Caterlytix Sales Narrative

Caterlytix should be positioned as the strategic platform business for fragmented hospitality and contract catering environments. The value proposition is not just integration. It is the combination of:

- Trac Core as middleware across fragmented source systems
- Trac ODM as the operational data layer with tenancy
- reporting, applications, and LLM-enabled solutions built on top of that foundation

The strongest commercial narrative is that Caterlytix helps large operators create order, visibility, and usable workflow capability without waiting for every source system to become standardised.

## Cross-Business Narrative

Where useful, the wider group can strengthen credibility:

- TAC Operations signals operating depth and shared specialist capability
- Pebble demonstrates direct product experience in another operational market
- ArroCMS shows broader SaaS and AI capability beyond one vertical
- AGENTSdotNE supports profile, network strength, and innovation adjacency

This should be used to add confidence, not to blur the customer-facing proposition of each business.

## What An AI Should Keep In Mind

- Lead with the business that is relevant to the buyer or stakeholder.
- Use the wider group narrative to support credibility, not to complicate the message.
- Keep the distinction clear between ownership, operating support, and customer-facing value.
- For Caterlytix, focus on middleware, data structure, operational visibility, and scalable application capability.
